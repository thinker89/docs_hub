# Another window to the market. Market Sentiment at a glance.
What is this app?
It’s a simple tool to quickly sense the mood of the U.S. stock market.

You can check sentiment indicators like Fear & Greed Index and the VIX in one place, without the clutter.
How do I use it?
Focus on three key signals: Fear & Greed, VIX, and the 10Y–2Y yield spread.

Turn on alerts to keep track of the shifts that matter to you.

Arrange the dashboard the way you like.

Note: This app does not provide investment advice. You are responsible for verifying the accuracy of the information displayed and for all investment decisions you make.
What is Fear & Greed?
It is a widely known indicator that makes it easy to grasp market sentiment. A value close to 0 indicates fear, while a value close to 100 indicates greed.
What is VIX?
The Market Volatility Index, often called the “fear gauge.”

It usually sits around 10–20, but spikes when markets turn chaotic.
What is yield curve inversion?
Normally, long-term bond yields are higher than short-term ones.

When the U.S. 10-year yield falls below the 2-year yield, the bond market is flashing a warning: recession risk ahead.

Historically, such inversions were often followed by shocks in the stock market.
