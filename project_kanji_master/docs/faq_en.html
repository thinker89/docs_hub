# FAQ

I made a purchase, but I can’t access all kanji
If you can’t access all kanji after purchasing, first fully close the app and restart it.
Also check that your network connection is stable.
If the issue persists, go to [Menu → Settings → Restore Purchases] and restore your purchase.
If you reinstalled the app or changed devices, you’ll also need to use [Restore Purchases].

Purchases can only be restored with the same Google Play or App Store account used for the purchase. If you used a different account, log into that account and restore your purchase.
No sound or the voice sounds robotic
This may happen if a Japanese voice is not installed on your device, or if a low-quality voice is selected.

Installing a high-quality Japanese voice will make pronunciation sound much more natural.

[iOS]
1. Open your device Settings.
2. Go to [Accessibility] → [Spoken Content] → [Voices].
3. Select [Japanese], then download a voice you like.
4. Make sure the downloaded Japanese voice is selected.

[Android]
1. Open your device Settings.
2. Use the search bar and type "Text-to-Speech" or "Text".
3. Open the Text-to-Speech settings.
4. Download a Japanese voice or switch to a high-quality Japanese voice.

After changing the settings, fully close the app and open it again.

If it still doesn’t work, your device may not support Japanese TTS.
I changed devices and my progress is gone
First, on your old device, go to [Menu → Settings → Save Progress] to create a backup.
Save the backup file somewhere you can access on your new device (messenger, iCloud, Google Drive, etc.).
On your new device, go to [Menu → Settings → Restore] and load the file to recover your progress.
I can’t see my saved progress file in cloud storage
The cloud storage shown in [Settings → Restore] may not always be up to date.
In this case, open your cloud app (such as iCloud or Google Drive) so your device can refresh and recognize the latest files.
It’s too easy—I want to skip levels
If the current level feels easy, it’s better to move on to the next one.

1) Tap [All Kanji] in the top menu
2) Tap the [⋮ menu] at the top right
3) Tap [Skip Level]

Skipped kanji will still appear occasionally in reviews, so don’t worry. If it feels easy, move on confidently.
Show me more features of the app
This app is designed so you can start learning right away by tapping “Start Learning,” without any complicated setup.
However, if you want more control, you can explore the following options:

1) Dark Mode
Switch the screen to a dark theme.

2) Word Priority Mode
Enable [Settings → Kanji Card Display → Word Priority] to show words first on the card.
If you’re an intermediate or advanced learner who wants to focus more on vocabulary than kanji stories, try this option.

3) Expand Examples by Default
If tapping to expand example sentences feels inconvenient, enable this option.

4) Adjust Study Amount
Each session shows 20 kanji cards by default, but you can adjust this to match your preferred study volume.

5) Save Learning Progress
Your progress is not stored on a server, so be sure to back it up regularly to your personal cloud.
Use this feature when switching to a new device as well.

6) View Weak Kanji
Go to [All Kanji → ⋮ menu → My Weak Kanji] to review kanji you struggle with.
Weak kanji are determined based on your difficulty responses.

7) Skip Current Level
If the current level feels too easy, you can skip it via [All Kanji → ⋮ menu → Skip Level].
Skipped kanji will still reappear occasionally to help reinforce your memory.
So don’t worry—feel free to skip easy levels with confidence.
Are kanji origins based on academic theory?
Some explanations follow traditional theories, while others are simplified or created to help you learn faster.
If you want academically accurate origins, refer to specialized resources or research materials.
Do you collect personal information?
This app does not collect personally identifiable information such as your name, email, or contact details.
However, technical data like usage patterns, learning progress, and error logs may be collected automatically to improve the service.

You can back up your learning data to your own Google Drive or iCloud if you wish. Any personal data collected by those services follows their respective policies.
Backup data includes app settings and learning progress, but not personal information like your name or email.
