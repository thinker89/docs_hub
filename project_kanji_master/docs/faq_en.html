# FAQ

I’m not receiving review notifications
Please turn on review notifications in Menu - Settings and make sure notification permission is enabled. If you open the app at least once on a given day, no notification will be sent that day. A notification will be sent again the next day.
Are the kanji origins based on academic theories?
Some kanji origin explanations on the cards are based on traditional academic theories, while others are creatively written to help you learn more efficiently. If you would like to study traditional theories in depth, please refer to specialized academic sources as well.
I want to save my learning progress
Before deleting the app or switching to another device, please use [Menu - Settings - Save learning progress] to back up your progress. Choosing iCloud or Google Drive as the storage option allows for more convenient backup and restore.

On the device where you want to restore your progress, use [Menu - Settings - Restore] and load the previously saved backup file.

If you delete the app without creating a backup, your app settings and learning progress will be lost and cannot be recovered.
I made a purchase but can’t access all kanji
If your payment was completed successfully but you still cannot access all kanji, please fully close the app and then reopen it.

If the issue persists, tap [Menu → Settings → Restore purchase] to restore your purchase history.

If you reinstalled the app or changed your device, please also use [Restore purchase].

Purchases can only be restored with the same Google Play or App Store account used for the original payment. If you paid with a different account, log in to the store with that account and then run Restore purchase.
Do you collect personal information?
This app does not collect personally identifiable information such as your name, email address, or contact details. For quality improvement purposes, technical analytics data such as usage patterns and learning progress may be collected automatically.

If you choose, you can back up your learning data to your own Google Drive or iCloud. Any personal data collected by those cloud services is governed by their respective policies. Backup data includes app settings and learning progress, but does not include personal information such as your name or email address.
