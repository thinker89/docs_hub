# The fastest way to master kanji. Japanese Kanji Master!

Memorize faster with the Cat Kanji Method
When you see a stray cat every day, you recognize it instantly—even if you don’t know details like “5 stripes” or “a white spot on its tail,” right?

From now on, learn kanji the same way you recognize familiar cats.

Stop trying to memorize exact shapes and stroke counts by writing them over and over.

────────

1) Tap "Start Study" and begin your daily session.

2) Don’t try to memorize every stroke or exact shape of a kanji. Just grasp the general form and move on quickly.

3) The app focuses on your weak kanji. From the next day, difficult kanji will appear more often, while mastered ones will appear less.

────────

With this method, you repeatedly encounter difficult kanji until they feel familiar — this is the Cat Kanji Method.
I’m curious about how kanji works
Think of English: in “dislike,” dis- means negation and like means to like, so together it means “to dislike.” In “preview,” pre- generally means “before.”

Similarly, kanji can be broken down into smaller components.

亻: person
木: tree
「休」: a person (亻) leaning on a tree (木) → means “rest”

石: means stone
更: represents the sound “こう” only
「硬」: means “hard like stone (石)” while 更 (こう) provides the sound

Components can represent meaning or sound, and most kanji can be understood this way.

Also, one component can have multiple meanings:

口: mouth, speech, eating, or even a square shape
十: ten, cross, or intersection

Just like you don’t memorize dis- or pre- separately, you don’t need to memorize components individually. As you study, you’ll naturally understand them.

Kanji difficulty levels
The difficulty levels follow how Japanese students learn kanji.

Levels 1–6: Elementary school kanji
Levels 7–8: Middle school kanji
Levels 9–10: High school kanji
Level 11: Common non-official kanji used in daily life

If you're preparing for the JLPT, use this as a guide:

N5: Level 1
N4: Levels 2–3
N3: Levels 4–5
N2: Levels 6–7
N1: Levels 8–9
High N1 score: All kanji
Check Weak Kanji
The app automatically brings back your weak kanji during daily study, so you don’t need to review them separately. However, if you want to review only your weak kanji before a test, follow these steps:

1) Tap [All Kanji] from the top menu on the title screen
2) Tap the [⋮ menu] at the top right of the [All Kanji] screen
3) Check [My Weak Kanji]
