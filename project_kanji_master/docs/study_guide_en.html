# The fastest way to master kanji. Japanese Kanji Master.

How to use the app
1) Tap the “Start Learning” button.

2) After checking the kanji, tap the “Show Answer” button at the bottom.

3) After reviewing the details, respond “Normal” or “Easy” for kanji you knew, and “Hard” or “Repeat” for difficult ones.

4) Get familiar with the overall shape of the kanji at a glance, then quickly move on to the next one. Don’t study too carefully for a long time—skim through them quickly, like flipping through a comic book.
How can I memorize them faster?
You don’t need to know the exact stroke count. Just get used to the general shape visually and move on quickly. Try to go through 20 kanji within 10 minutes.

Make sure to learn the example words together with the kanji.

Studying a large amount at once is less effective than reviewing a small amount every day.
There are kanji I just can’t memorize
If a kanji won’t stick, mark it as “Hard” and move on quickly.

Kanji marked as “Hard” will appear again in the next day’s review.

If you keep reviewing daily, you’ll suddenly realize you remember it.

For complex kanji with many strokes, don’t try to memorize the exact stroke count—just grasp the overall shape and get used to it visually.
The Structure of Kanji
Kanji can be broken down into smaller components. Let’s look at some examples.

亻: means “person.”
木: means “tree.”
「休」: A person (亻) leaning against a tree (木) to rest. It means “rest.”

石: means “stone.”
更: does not have a specific meaning here and only indicates the sound “こう.”
「硬」: Means “hard,” like a stone (石). 更 (こう) represents the sound only, without adding meaning.

In this way, components express either meaning or sound. Most kanji can be understood quickly through this principle.

Also, a single component can have multiple interpretations.

口: means “mouth,” but can also represent speech or a square-shaped object.
十: means “ten,” but can also represent a cross shape or an intersection.

There is no need to memorize components separately. As you continue learning with this app, you will naturally become familiar with them.
Automatic review system
The app remembers your responses and automatically schedules which kanji to review and when.

So there’s no need to create your own review plan.

Trust the automatic review system and study consistently every day to reinforce your memory.
Should I practice writing kanji?
No. It’s not recommended. Training yourself to quickly recognize the overall shape of kanji is much more effective.

In modern Japanese, most writing is done through keyboard input with automatic conversion. Strong reading ability naturally supports writing ability.

Unless your job requires handwriting kanji or you practice calligraphy as a hobby, focus on reading practice.
Kanji difficulty levels
The difficulty levels in this app follow the classification of the official Japanese Kanji Proficiency Test.

Level 1–6: Elementary school kanji
Level 7–8: Middle school kanji
Level 9–10: High school kanji
Level 11: Commonly used non-official kanji
