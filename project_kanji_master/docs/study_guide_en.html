# The fastest way to master kanji. Japanese Kanji Master!

Memorize faster with the Cat Kanji Method
When you see a stray cat every day, you recognize it instantly—even if you don’t know details like “5 stripes” or “a white spot on its tail,” right?

From now on, learn kanji the same way you recognize familiar cats.

Stop trying to memorize exact shapes and stroke counts by writing them over and over.

────────

1) Tap “Start Learning.”

2) Glance quickly at the kanji, then tap “Show Answer.”

3) Review the story, illustration, and key words.

4) If it was hard, tap “Hard.” If not, tap “Normal” or “Easy.”

────────

The app remembers your responses.
“Hard” kanji will appear more often.
“Easy” kanji will appear less frequently.

This method helps you become familiar with difficult kanji by seeing them repeatedly—this is the Cat Kanji Method.
Some kanji just won’t stick
If a kanji doesn’t stick after a few tries, mark it as “Hard” and move on quickly.

Kanji marked “Hard” will come back in review.
As you see them repeatedly every day, they will eventually stick naturally.

For complex kanji, don’t try to memorize exact stroke counts. Just get a rough sense of the shape and get familiar with it. Daily repetition is key.
Automatic review
The app remembers your responses and schedules reviews at optimal intervals.

So you don’t need to plan your own review schedule.

Trust the automatic review system and study consistently every day to reinforce your memory.
Review your weak kanji
Before a test or interview, you may want to quickly review only your weak kanji. You can gather them like this:

1) Tap [All Kanji] in the top menu
2) Tap the [⋮ menu] at the top right
3) Select [My Weak Kanji]

The app tracks your responses and classifies weak kanji automatically. Normally, just follow regular learning, and use this feature only when needed.
Kanji difficulty levels
The difficulty levels follow how Japanese students learn kanji.

Levels 1–6: Elementary school kanji
Levels 7–8: Middle school kanji
Levels 9–10: High school kanji
Level 11: Common non-official kanji used in daily life

If you're preparing for the JLPT, use this as a guide:

N5: Level 1
N4: Levels 2–3
N3: Levels 4–5
N2: Levels 6–7
N1: Levels 8–9
High N1 score: All kanji
